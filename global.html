UNESCO World Heritage Nomination Strategies

Since 1978, nations have inscribed over 1,100 sites on the UNESCO World Heritage List. But they use two fundamentally different strategies for presenting cultural heritage:

Ensemble nominations (red) inscribe entire historic cities or cultural landscapes, embedding religious and secular monuments within a broader urban or territorial narrative.

Individual monuments (gray) isolate single buildings or archaeological sites, emphasizing their architectural or symbolic uniqueness.

This atlas examines how post-Soviet Central Asian states — particularly Uzbekistan and Kazakhstan — employed these strategies differently when presenting Islamic heritage to UNESCO in the 1990s-2000s.

Notice the concentration of ensemble nominations in Europe, the Middle East, and East Asia — regions with established traditions of presenting religious architecture within urban frameworks.
