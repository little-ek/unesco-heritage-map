Global UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The 1,247 UNESCO World Heritage Sites span every continent and represent humanity's shared cultural and natural heritage. But how nations frame these sites reveals strategic choices about identity and presentation.

This map shows five distinct nomination approaches, with ensemble nominations (437 sites, 43% of cultural sites) being the most common strategy for presenting cultural heritage.

Notice how ensemble nominations cluster in Latin America (51%), the Arab world (47%), and Europe (44%) — regions with traditions of presenting heritage within urban or landscape contexts rather than as isolated monuments.

Nomination Strategies (Priority Hierarchy):

Note: When sites have multiple characteristics (e.g., both ensemble and transnational), they're colored by the highest priority category. Ensembles take precedence as they're most relevant to this atlas's argument.
