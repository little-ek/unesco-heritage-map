Global UNESCO World Heritage Sites

The 1,247 UNESCO World Heritage Sites span every continent and represent humanity's shared cultural and natural heritage. But how nations frame these sites reveals strategic choices about identity and presentation. An "ensemble" refers to a grouping of buildings or architectural elements that gain their significance from their collective relationship‚Äîthrough their architectural coherence, their homogeneity, or their placement within a landscape‚Äîrather than from individual monuments standing alone. This is particularly relevant for historic urban centers and architectural complexes where the value lies in the overall composition rather than isolated structures.

This map shows five distinct nomination approaches, with ensemble nominations (437 sites, 43% of cultural sites) being the most common strategy for presenting cultural heritage.
