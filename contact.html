Acknowledgments
This interactive atlas was developed as part of a GIS for Design course at Columbia University. The project examines how inherited institutional frameworks shape UNESCO World Heritage nomination strategies, with particular focus on post-Soviet ensemble approaches.
Special thanks to:

Next Steps for Research
This atlas represents preliminary spatial analysis for a larger thesis project. Future research directions include:
  • Archival Research in Uzbekistan: Examine УзНІІП (Uzbek Scientific Research Institute) documentation of ensemble nomination preparation (1988-1993)
  • Comparative Property Analysis: Map Soviet-era museum-reserve boundaries against UNESCO inscription boundaries to assess framework continuity
  • Statistical Expansion: Extend ensemble analysis to all 1,247 UNESCO sites with formal classification system
  • Policy Recommendations: Develop operational definitions for ensemble categories in UNESCO guidelines
  • Publication: Submit findings to International Journal of Heritage Studies or Change Over Time
Data Sources & Methods
Primary Data: UNESCO World Heritage Centre Syndication (2025)
Tools: MapLibre GL JS, Python (pandas, geojson), Claude.ai
Classification: Ensemble sites identified through textual analysis of UNESCO short descriptions and nomination criteria
Scope: 80 sites across 15 former USSR states; 1,247 global sites for comparative analysis
